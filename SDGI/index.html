Career path of excitement

What makes a job feel like work? In my opinion, not having a genuine passion for what you do is what can make a job feel like work. For me, computer science and software has always been a dream of mine. The only thing that held me back was my fear of code and this foreign laugage. That moment of anxiety when you accidentally hit the F12 button and these array of numbers, code, and commands that came up seemed to me as an impossible feat to learn. As i grew older my fear slowly deminished. Today i still am a bit intimifated bythe advancements but with this course i feel i will have the tools to pursue and excel in a career in software engineering.

As the years pass, the ease of use for website and app development will bring down the demand for software engineers. User interfaces can and will replace third party developers. For now, coders, designers, and those who are pursing IT are the future of the standard operating funtionality of the world. This is why i chose to study and purse a career in coding.
